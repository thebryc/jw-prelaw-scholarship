About the Scholarship

Investing in Louisiana's Legal Future

The Jones Walker Pre-Law Scholarship provides funding to BRYC College Fellows and alumni who demonstrate an interest in attending law school or furthering their education to pursue careers in the legal field. Jones Walker LLP is one of the largest law firms in the American South, with a deep commitment to developing the next generation of legal talent in Louisiana and beyond.

BRYC and Jones Walker share a belief that diverse, well-prepared professionals strengthen our legal community — and that access to opportunity should not be determined by circumstance. This scholarship is one way we make that belief real.

Eligibility

Who Can Apply

  • You are a current BRYC Fellow (high school senior) or a BRYC alumnus/alumna.
  • You are actively enrolled in a postsecondary institution, or are planning to enroll (or re-enroll) in the upcoming academic year.
  • You are pursuing a career in the legal community — including but not limited to law school, criminal justice, public policy, civil rights, or paralegal studies.
  • You are willing to attend a spring reception hosted by Jones Walker to celebrate scholarship recipients.
Scholarship Restrictions

Before You Apply

  • You must be in good standing with BRYC — meaning a minimum of 80% program attendance, or responsiveness to the Persistence Team's outreach.
  • You can only receive one BRYC scholarship per academic year. This includes the Albert Fraenkel Award for Alumni.
  • You cannot receive the same BRYC scholarship two years in a row.
  • You cannot receive the same BRYC scholarship more than twice.

About the Scholarship

The Jones Walker Pre-Law Scholarship provides funding to BRYC College Fellows and alumni who demonstrate an interest in attending law school or furthering their education to pursue careers in the legal field. This scholarship reflects a shared conviction between BRYC and Jones Walker that diverse, well-prepared professionals strengthen Louisiana's legal community — and that access to opportunity should not be determined by circumstance.

You are part of the committee that will help identify this year's four recipients: students and alumni who show genuine purpose, preparation, and commitment to a career in the legal field.

Your Charge as a Reviewer
1
Read the full essay before scoring. Resist the urge to skim. The rubric rewards depth, and depth often reveals itself slowly. These applicants worked hard on these essays.
2
Score what's there, not what's missing. A high school senior and a BRYC alumnus will naturally write very differently. Evaluate each applicant against what you would expect for where they are in their journey — not against each other.
3
Look for purpose over polish. We are identifying students who show genuine commitment to a legal career, not just strong writers. A student who clearly knows why they want to serve their community matters more than a perfectly structured essay.
4
Be consistent. Hold the same standard across all applicants in a group. If you give one person a 3 for Interest, make sure you're applying that same bar to everyone.
5
Your scores are confidential. Reviewers do not see each other's scores during the process. Only the admin sees aggregate results. Score honestly and independently.
6
When in doubt, trust the rubric. The four categories — Interest, Engagement, Purpose, and Writing Mechanics — are designed to surface the whole applicant, not just their writing ability. Let the criteria guide you.
